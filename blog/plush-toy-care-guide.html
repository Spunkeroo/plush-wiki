The Complete Guide to Cleaning and Caring for Plush Toys

Published February 23, 2026 • 15 min read • By Plush Wiki

Plush toys accumulate dust, dirt, allergens, and the general wear of being loved. Whether it is a child's best friend that has seen better days, a collector's piece that needs careful maintenance, or a vintage find that deserves restoration, knowing how to properly clean and care for plush toys extends their life and keeps them looking their best.

This comprehensive guide covers every cleaning method, from quick spot treatments to full machine washes, plus repair techniques, storage best practices, and long-term care tips for every type of plush toy.

Before You Clean: Assessment and Preparation

Check the Care Label

Always start with the care label if one exists. Most modern plush toys include washing instructions. Look for symbols indicating machine washable, hand wash only, surface wash only, or dry clean only. If the label is missing or illegible, err on the side of the gentlest method that will accomplish your cleaning goal.

Inspect the Toy

Before any cleaning method, examine the plush carefully:

  • Loose seams: Repair any open or weak seams before washing. Water will worsen them
  • Loose parts: Check that eyes, noses, buttons, and accessories are securely attached
  • Electronic components: Identify and remove any battery packs, sound boxes, or electronic modules if possible
  • Delicate materials: Note any special fabrics, glued-on decorations, or painted features that might not survive water
  • Stains: Identify stain types (food, ink, mud, etc.) so you can pre-treat them appropriately

Determine the Cleaning Level Needed

  • Light dust and freshening: Surface cleaning or dry methods
  • Spot stains: Targeted spot cleaning
  • General dirt and odor: Hand washing or machine washing
  • Deep cleaning and sanitizing: Full wash with appropriate disinfection

Method 1: Surface Cleaning (Safest for All Plush)

Surface cleaning is the gentlest approach and is safe for virtually all plush toys, including vintage, antique, electronic, and delicate pieces.

Dry Dusting

  1. Take the plush toy outside if possible to avoid spreading dust indoors
  2. Use a lint roller to remove surface dust, hair, and debris
  3. For deeper fur, use a soft-bristled brush (a clean toothbrush works for small areas)
  4. Gently brush in the direction of the fur pile
  5. For stubborn debris, use a vacuum cleaner with an upholstery attachment on the lowest suction setting

Damp Cloth Wipe

  1. Dampen a clean white cloth with cool water (white to avoid dye transfer)
  2. Add a tiny drop of mild dish soap or baby shampoo to the cloth
  3. Gently wipe the surface of the plush, working in small sections
  4. Follow with a clean damp cloth (no soap) to remove residue
  5. Blot with a dry towel and allow to air dry completely
Freshening Tip: To remove odors without washing, sprinkle baking soda generously over the plush toy, place it in a sealed plastic bag, and leave it for 8-12 hours (overnight is ideal). Remove and shake or vacuum off the baking soda. This absorbs odors remarkably well.

Method 2: Spot Cleaning

For isolated stains that do not require a full wash.

General Stain Removal

  1. Mix a small amount of mild liquid detergent with cool water to create suds
  2. Using a clean white cloth, apply only the suds (not the liquid) to the stained area
  3. Gently blot and dab. Never rub, as rubbing can damage the plush fibers and spread the stain
  4. Rinse the area by blotting with a clean cloth dampened with plain water
  5. Repeat until the stain is gone or no longer improving
  6. Blot dry with a clean towel and air dry completely

Specific Stain Treatments

  • Food stains: Pre-treat with a paste of baking soda and water. Let it sit for 15 minutes, then spot clean normally
  • Ink stains: Dab with rubbing alcohol on a cotton swab. Test on a hidden area first to check for color fastness
  • Blood stains: Use cold water only (hot water sets blood stains). Hydrogen peroxide works on white or light-colored plush, but test first
  • Grease and oil: Apply cornstarch to absorb the grease. Let it sit for an hour, brush off, then spot clean with dish soap
  • Mud: Let the mud dry completely first. Brush off as much as possible, then spot clean the remainder

Method 3: Hand Washing

Hand washing is the safest way to give a plush toy a thorough cleaning while maintaining control over the process.

  1. Fill a clean basin or sink with cool to lukewarm water (never hot)
  2. Add a small amount of mild detergent or baby shampoo. A teaspoon is usually enough
  3. Submerge the plush toy and gently squeeze the soapy water through it. Avoid twisting or wringing
  4. For stubborn areas, use a soft cloth or toothbrush to gently scrub
  5. Drain the soapy water and refill with clean, cool water
  6. Rinse by gently squeezing the toy in clean water. Repeat until all soap is removed
  7. Gently press (do not wring) excess water out of the toy
  8. Roll the toy in a clean, dry towel to absorb additional moisture
  9. Reshape the toy and air dry completely (see drying section below)
Warning: Never hand wash plush toys with electronic components that cannot be removed, glued-on features that may dissolve, or antique and vintage pieces where fabric integrity is uncertain. Use surface cleaning for these.

Method 4: Machine Washing

Machine washing is the most convenient method for sturdy, modern plush toys that are labeled machine washable.

Step-by-Step Machine Washing

  1. Pre-treat stains: Spot treat any visible stains before machine washing
  2. Protect the toy: Place the plush in a zippered mesh laundry bag or pillowcase tied shut. This prevents snagging and protects delicate features
  3. Select settings: Use the gentle or delicate cycle with cold water. Hot water can shrink, fade, or damage plush fabric
  4. Choose detergent: Use a mild, fragrance-free liquid detergent. Avoid bleach and fabric softener
  5. Wash solo or with like items: Wash the plush alone or with similar soft items (towels work as padding)
  6. Remove promptly: Take the toy out as soon as the cycle ends to prevent musty odors
  7. Reshape and dry: Reshape immediately and proceed to drying
Machine Wash Safe: Most Squishmallows, modern Jellycat toys, Build-A-Bear animals (with sound boxes removed), and mass-market plush toys can handle gentle machine washing. When in doubt, hand wash.

Drying Your Plush Toys

Air Drying (Recommended)

Air drying is the safest method for all plush toys:

  • Reshape the toy while still damp, positioning limbs, ears, and features correctly
  • Place on a clean, dry towel in a well-ventilated area
  • Avoid direct sunlight, which can fade colors
  • Turn the toy periodically to ensure even drying
  • Fluff the fur gently with a soft brush as it dries
  • Allow 24-48 hours for complete drying depending on size and humidity

Machine Drying (Use with Caution)

Only machine dry plush toys that are specifically labeled as dryer safe:

  • Use the lowest heat setting or air fluff (no heat) cycle
  • Keep the toy in its mesh laundry bag
  • Add a couple of dry towels to the dryer for cushioning
  • Check every 10-15 minutes to prevent overheating
  • Remove while slightly damp and finish with air drying
Important: A plush toy must be completely dry before storage or use. Any remaining moisture trapped inside the filling creates a breeding ground for mold and mildew, which can ruin the toy and pose health risks.

Basic Plush Toy Repairs

Fixing Loose Seams

Seam splits are the most common plush repair. You will need a needle, thread matching the plush color, and about 10 minutes:

  1. Thread the needle and knot the end
  2. Start stitching from inside the opening (push the needle from inside to outside) to hide the knot
  3. Use a ladder stitch (also called an invisible stitch) for the cleanest result: alternate between small stitches on each side of the opening
  4. Pull gently after every few stitches to close the gap
  5. Knot and bury the thread end inside the plush

Replacing Stuffing

If a plush has become flat or lumpy, you can refresh the filling:

  • Open a seam in an inconspicuous area (under an arm, along the back)
  • Remove old, clumped filling
  • Add new polyester fiberfill in small amounts, distributing evenly
  • Stuff firmly but not tight; overstuffed plush loses its huggable quality
  • Close the seam with a ladder stitch

Reattaching Eyes and Noses

For safety-style plastic eyes and noses with posts, you will need replacement safety eyes (available at craft stores). For sewn-on features, simply re-stitch with matching thread. For glued features, use fabric-safe adhesive and allow 24 hours to cure before handling.

Long-Term Care and Storage

Regular Maintenance

  • Weekly: Lint roll displayed plush to prevent dust accumulation
  • Monthly: Shake or lightly vacuum plush toys, especially those in children's rooms
  • Quarterly: Deep clean frequently used plush toys (machine or hand wash)
  • Annually: Inspect all plush for loose seams, worn areas, and needed repairs

Storage Best Practices

  • Clean before storing: Always wash and completely dry plush before long-term storage
  • Use breathable containers: Cotton bags, acid-free tissue paper, or ventilated plastic bins. Avoid sealed plastic bags that trap moisture
  • Avoid attics and basements: Temperature extremes and humidity are enemies of plush. Choose a climate-controlled closet or room
  • Protect from sunlight: UV rays fade colors over time. Store away from windows
  • Include cedar blocks or lavender sachets: Natural moth deterrents that also keep storage smelling fresh
  • Do not stack heavy items on top: Compression permanently flattens filling over time
A well-maintained plush toy can last for decades or even generations. The time you invest in proper care today preserves the softness, appearance, and emotional value for years to come.

Caring for Special Plush Types

Vintage and Antique Plush

Vintage plush requires extra caution. The materials may be fragile, dyes may not be colorfast, and original filling may be excelsior (wood shavings) or kapok rather than polyester. Surface cleaning only is recommended unless you are experienced with textile restoration. For valuable vintage pieces, consult a professional textile conservator.

Weighted Plush Toys

Many weighted plush contain glass microbeads or poly pellets that add weight. These can usually be machine washed, but always use a laundry bag to contain any beads if a seam opens. Check seam integrity carefully before washing.

Electronic and Interactive Plush

Remove all electronic components before any water-based cleaning. If electronics are not removable, surface clean only. Some electronic plush have removable fabric shells that can be washed separately.

Allergen Management

Plush toys can harbor dust mites, a common trigger for allergies and asthma. For households with allergy sufferers:

  • Freeze treatment: Place the plush in a sealed bag and freeze for 24 hours. This kills dust mites. Follow with a wash or thorough vacuuming to remove the dead mites
  • Hot wash when possible: Water above 130F (55C) kills dust mites. Only use this on plush labeled for hot water washing
  • Reduce plush in bedrooms: Limit the number of stuffed animals on beds. One or two favorites is better than a pile for allergy management
  • Encase frequently: Use allergen-proof covers on plush that live on beds
  • Regular washing schedule: Wash or freeze-treat bedroom plush every two to four weeks during allergy seasons

Conclusion

Caring for plush toys is straightforward once you know the right methods for the right situations. Surface cleaning handles everyday dust and freshening. Spot cleaning tackles individual stains. Hand washing provides thorough cleaning with gentle control. Machine washing handles the heavy lifting for sturdy, modern toys. And proper storage and repair keep your plush looking their best for years to come.

The key principles are simple: start with the gentlest method, always test on a hidden area first, dry completely before storage, and repair promptly before small issues become big problems.
